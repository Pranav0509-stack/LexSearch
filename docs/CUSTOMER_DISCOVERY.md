# Sanhita AI — Customer Discovery Playbook

**Living doc.** Update after every conversation. The goal is to know more about Indian CAs and lawyers than anyone else building tools for them.

> 📌 How to import to Google Docs: open Google Docs → File → Open → Upload → drop this `.md`. Or copy-paste — formatting carries over. For Notion: drag the `.md` into a page. For Obsidian: drop it in your vault.

---

## 🎯 What we're learning (and why)

Three questions decide whether Sanhita succeeds:

1. **Is the pain real?** Are CAs and lawyers actually losing hours/week to bad research tools — or is "Taxmann is fine" the truth?
2. **Are they willing to switch?** Switching costs (habit, firm contracts, training) often beat product superiority.
3. **What is the wedge feature?** The one thing Sanhita does that gets the first hundred firms to leave Taxmann/Manupatra.

If we don't have crisp answers to these after 20 conversations, we're building blind.

---

## 📐 First Principles (The "Mom Test" Rules)

1. **Ask about their life, not your idea.** "What did you do last Friday?" beats "would you pay for X?"
2. **Past behavior > future intent.** "Show me the last scrutiny notice you replied to" beats "would you use a scrutiny drafter?"
3. **Specific > general.** "Hours on the Mehta matter last week?" beats "how much time on research?"
4. **Compliments are noise.** Treat "this looks great" as a polite no until they pull out a real document.
5. **Money talks louder than words.** Every meeting must end with one of: free-trial signup, real document shared, scheduled follow-up, or referral.

---

## ⭐ The 7 Questions That Matter Most

When the meeting goes off the rails and you have 5 minutes left:

1. **Walk me through the last time you spent more than 2 hours on research. What happened?**
2. **What tool would you cancel if it wasn't free anymore?**
3. **What's the most expensive software your firm pays for?**
4. **Last time a client asked you something and you said "let me get back to you" — what was it?**
5. **If I built a tool that did X for you, what's the first feature you'd ask me to add?**
6. **Who in your network would hate Taxmann/Manupatra as much as you do?**
7. **Can I send you a free trial tonight, watch you use it next week?**

## 🗂️ Question Bank — Full Set

Use these as a menu. Don't ask them all in one meeting — pick 8-10 based on which section is least understood for that person.

### A. Validate the problem exists

| # | Question |
|---|---|
| A1 | Walk me through your day yesterday — actual hour by hour. |
| A2 | What was the last research question you Googled or searched in Taxmann? |
| A3 | What was the last thing you stayed late at work for? |
| A4 | What's the most annoying thing about how you do your work today? |
| A5 | If I gave you 2 extra hours every day, what would you stop doing? |
| A6 | Last time a client called and asked something on the spot — what happened? |
| A7 | Tell me about the worst scrutiny notice / appeal / case you handled recently. |
| A8 | Who in your firm wastes the most time on research? |

### B. Map their workflow

| # | Question |
|---|---|
| B1 | When you get a [scrutiny notice / brief / drafting task], what are the first 5 things you do? |
| B2 | Show me your bookmarks bar / open tabs right now. |
| B3 | Walk me through Taxmann/Manupatra search — what do you type, what do you click? |
| B4 | When was the last time a search didn't find what you needed? What did you do next? |
| B5 | Where do you save the precedents you find? |
| B6 | How do you keep up with new circulars / notifications? |
| B7 | Who do you call when you're stuck on a research question? |
| B8 | Show me the last reply / brief / opinion you drafted — how much was original vs copy-paste? |
| B9 | How do you track citations across a long matter — say, 20 cases for one client? |

### C. Tools and cost

| # | Question |
|---|---|

### D. Willingness to pay

| # | Question |
|---|---|

### E. Feature deep-dive (only after A–D)

| # | Question | Tests |
|---|---|---|
| E1 | If we showed you every CBDT/CBIC notification with smart filters — would you cancel any newsletter? | Notification firehose |

## 🚦 Read-the-room Signals

Pay extra attention:

| Signal | What it means |
|---|---|
| "We" instead of "I" | They're not the buyer — find who is |
| Checking phone during demo | Not engaged — change tactic or wrap |
| "What does it cost?" in first 5 min | Price-sensitive — anchor low |
| "What about [obscure feature]?" | Power user — keep close |
| "Interesting" twice | Polite no |
| Pulls out laptop and tries it | Real interest — gold |
| Sends real document afterward | True buyer signal |
| Forwards to colleague unprompted | Referral engine started |
| Asks about data security in detail | Big firm; can pay; will demand SOC2 |
| Asks about API / integrations | Sophisticated; long sales cycle, high LTV |

## ❌ Questions to NEVER Ask

| Don't ask | Why |
|---|---|
| "Do you like our product?" | They lie politely |
| "Would you pay for this?" | Future-intent bias |
| "How much would you pay?" | Anchoring; lowballs |
| "Is this useful?" | Empty signal |
| "Cool right?" | Begging for validation |
| "Don't you think this beats Taxmann?" | Leading — invalid data |

## 🎯 Decision points (when to pivot vs stay the course)

After every 10 conversations, ask:

- **If <30% mention research-tool pain in top 3:** problem may not be P0. Consider repositioning or different ICP.
- **If >70% say "Indian Kanoon is fine":** the wedge isn't "free Indian Kanoon" — it has to be a feature paid tools also lack.
- **If 0 paid pilots after 15 conversations:** something is wrong with the ask, the price, or the product. Don't keep scaling top-of-funnel without diagnosing.
- **If 4+ ask for the same missing feature:** stop everything else and build that.
