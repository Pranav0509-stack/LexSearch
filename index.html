When does a writ of mandamus lie against a private body?
A writ of mandamus is ordinarily against a public authority, but the Supreme Court has held it lies against a private body performing a public duty [1]. The test is the nature of the function, not the identity of the body [2].

Practice note: plead the public-duty element with specificity; a bare "public importance" won't carry.
